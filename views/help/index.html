Cron expression description

The Cron expression is a string, The strings are separated by spaces, Is divided into 5 or 6 domains, each domain represents a meaning, The system supports the following expressions：

Seconds Minutes Hours DayofMonth Month [DayofWeek]
DayofWeek is optional

The characters that can appear for each field are as follows：

  • Seconds: Can appear "* /, -" four characters, The valid range is an integer from 0-59
  • Minutes: There may be "* /, -" four characters, the effective range of 0-59 integer
  • Hours: There may be "* /, -" four characters, the effective range of 0-23 integer
  • DayofMonth: Can appear "* /, -?" Five characters, the effective range of 0-31 integer
  • Month: Can appear, "- * /" four characters, an integer ranging from 1-12 or JAN-DEC
  • DayofWeek: Can appear "* /, -?" Five characters, the effective range of 0-6 integer or SUN-SAT two range. 0 for Sunday, 1 for Monday, and so on

Each character has the following meaning：

  • * Represents any value that matches the field, If you use * in the Minutes field, This means that the event is triggered every minute.
  • ? Characters are used only for the day (month) and day (week) two sub-expressions, Indicates that no value is specified, When one of the two subexpressions is assigned a value, In order to avoid conflicts, You need to set the value of another subexpression to "?".
  • - Representation range, for example, in the Minutes domain 5-20, Indicates a trigger from 5 minutes to 20 minutes per minute.
  • / Indicates that the start time starts to trigger, And then triggered once every fixed time, For example, in the Minutes domain using 5/20, it means that 5 minutes to trigger once, And 25,45, respectively, triggered once.
  • , Indicates enumeration value values are listed. For example: in the Minutes domain using 5,20, It means triggering at 5 and 20 minutes per minute.

Some examples： 

0 0 10,14,16 * * ? Every day at 10:00, 14:00, 16:00

0 0/30 9-17 * * ? Every half hour between 09:00-17:00

0 0 12 ? * WED Every Wednesday at 12 noon

0 0 12 * * ? Every day at 12:00

0 15 10 ? * * Every day at 10:15 in the morning

0 15 10 * * ? Every day at 10:15 in the morning

0 15 10 * * ? * Every day at 10:15 in the morning

0 * 14 * * ? Every 1 minute between 14:00 and 14:59 daily

0 0/5 14 * * ? Every 5 minutes between 14:00 and 14:55 daily

0 0/5 14,18 * * ? Every 5 minutes between 14:00 to 14:55 and 18:00 to 18:55

0 0-5 14 * * ? Every 1 minute between 14:00 and 14:05 daily

0 10,44 14 ? 3 WED Every Wednesday on March at 14:10 and 14:44

0 15 10 ? * MON-FRI Triggered at 10:15 Monday through Friday

0 15 10 15 * ? Every month on the 15th at 10:15

0 15 10 L * ? Trigger on the last day of the month at 10:15
